01

Sovereignty

Your identity is an Ethereum wallet — something you generate and control. No email, no phone number, no company database storing who you are.

02

Decentralization

Messages travel through the Streamr Network. Each channel forms its own peer-to-peer overlay — a constellation of the nodes participating in the conversation. There is no central server to hack, censor, or shut down.

03

Transparency

Every line of code is open source. The encryption runs in your browser using standard Web APIs. Nothing is hidden, everything is verifiable.

Ownership

Channels belong to their creators.

When you create a channel in Pombo, you're creating a data stream recorded on the Polygon blockchain with a smart contract. Your wallet holds the on-chain ownership and permissions. Pombo is just the interface; the channel is yours.

You set the rules. Choose who can join, whether messages are encrypted, and how long history is stored.

You hold the keys. Your channels are tied to your wallet. No one — including Pombo — can take them away or alter permissions.

Pombo is a gateway. If Pombo disappears tomorrow, your channels still exist on the network. Any compatible client can access them.

Direct Messages

Message anyone.
Just an address.

Send end-to-end encrypted messages to any Ethereum address. No friend request, no mutual contacts needed — if you know someone's address, you can reach them.

Encrypted by default. Both wallets independently compute a shared secret. No one else can derive it — not even the network nodes.

No social graph. Your inbox is public-write, owner-read. The network can't see who's talking to you.

Push notifications that respect your privacy — anonymous relays wake your device without knowing who the message is for.
