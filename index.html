Ownership

Channels belong to their creators.

When you create a channel in Pombo, you're creating a smart contract on the Polygon blockchain. Your account holds the on-chain ownership and permissions. Pombo is just the interface; the channel is yours.

You set the rules. Choose who can join, whether messages are encrypted, and how long history is stored.

You hold the keys. Your channels are tied to your wallet. No one — including Pombo — can take them away or alter permissions.

Pombo is a gateway. If Pombo disappears tomorrow, your channels still exist on the network. Any compatible client can access them.

Direct Messages

Message anyone.
Just an address.

Send end-to-end encrypted messages to any Ethereum address. No friend requests, no mutual contacts — just an address.

Your DM inbox works like a mailbox. Anyone can write to you; only you can read it. A one-time on-chain registration (small fee in POL) creates two streams tied to your address — one for stored data, one ephemeral.

E2E encrypted with ECDH. Both wallets independently compute a shared secret. Messages are encrypted with AES-256-GCM — not even the network nodes can read them.

Stored locally + on storage nodes. Sent DM messages are saved encrypted on your device. Received messages are kept on storage nodes. Both merge seamlessly when you open a conversation.

Cross-device sync. Your inbox doubles as a sync channel — encrypted state is published to your own stream and fetched by any new device you sign in on.

How It Works

Two streams.
Zero servers.

Every channel uses two separate data streams on the Streamr Network — one for messages that are saved, and one for live-only signals like typing indicators and file transfers.

When you send a message, it's published to the network and delivered to everyone in the channel in real-time — no central server involved. Your message history is kept on a cluster of storage nodes of your choice.

Live signals — like who's online, who's typing, and peer-to-peer file transfers — travel through a separate ephemeral stream that leaves no trace.

Why two streams?

Keeping live signals separate from saved messages means your typing indicators and online status are never stored anywhere. Files are transferred directly between peers, without ever touching storage nodes.

Security

Encryption you
can verify.

All encryption happens in your browser using built-in browser APIs. No external libraries, no hidden servers. The source code is open for anyone to audit.

Password Channels

Your password is transformed into a strong encryption key using 310,000 rounds of key stretching, making brute-force attacks impractical.

Direct Messages

DMs use key agreement between your wallet and the recipient's — both sides independently compute a shared secret that no one else can derive.

Wallet Storage

Your private key is stored locally on your device, encrypted with your passphrase. It never leaves your browser.

// Password channels
password → PBKDF2 (310k rounds)
  → 256-bit encryption key

// Direct messages
your wallet + their wallet → ECDH
  → shared secret → AES-GCM

// Every message
AES-GCM + unique IV
  → encrypted + tamper-proof

// Wallet (local only)
passphrase → scrypt → AES
  → encrypted key on your device

All cryptographic operations run in your browser via window.crypto.subtle
